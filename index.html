Me

Obsidian is part of my name for a reason. Sculk contains and obsidian is tuff/endurant/hard. Although my MC skin is white, my last one wasn't but for simplicities sake, and also to make it more recognizable, I made a white and black skin. The white color also stands out in contrast to the background, which is great for videos were I'm the focus point but bad for hiding or standing out in a snowy biome.

I prefer detailed skins but the simpler option is often the better option for memory and makes me look for a deeper meaning.

I have multiple MC skins but 2 versions specifically for the channel skin. One version has shadows and exists mainly for this website. Programming a shading system would be too much work, so simply adding the shadows to the skin makes it so much easier to showcase my skin on a website without making it look like a white stick-figure.

The unshaded skin is for videos since Minecraft already shades the environment, including the skin. Shading the skin is only important because the skin is mainly white and the colors would blend together and make the skin look like a white blob if it wasn't shaded.
